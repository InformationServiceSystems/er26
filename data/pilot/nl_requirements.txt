The order management process centers on customers, their orders, and the products they purchase. Each customer is identified by a short company code and a company name. A customer may place multiple orders over time, though some customers may not have placed any orders yet. Every order is associated with exactly one customer, and each order is recorded with the date it was placed. An order is made up of one or more individual line items, each of which specifies a particular product along with the quantity requested and the agreed-upon unit price for that item. A single order must always contain at least one line item; an order with no items would not be meaningful. Different line items within the same order may refer to different products, and the same product may appear across many different orders placed by various customers.

Products in the catalog are tracked by name and may be flagged as discontinued when they are no longer offered for sale. A discontinued product should not be added to any new order, although historical orders that included the product before it was discontinued remain valid and unchanged. The business needs visibility into which products are active and which have been retired, so that staff processing new orders can avoid selecting items that are no longer available. Overall, the system should ensure that every order is tied to a known customer, that each line item points to a recognized product, and that the quantities and prices recorded on each line item are sensible and complete.
