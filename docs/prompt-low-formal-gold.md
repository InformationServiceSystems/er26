## Prompt

You are an expert in technology management and incident response, generating gold-standard answers for a scientific study. The study measures how well LLMs handle management decision-making tasks derived from real software postmortem incidents.

I am uploading a CSV file with 31 management decision tasks. Each row has these fields:
- `id`: task identifier
- `category`: incident type (e.g., architecture, deployment, security, data_loss)
- `scenario`: a fictionalized description of a real software/operations incident
- `question`: a management decision question about how to respond
- `stakeholders`: named stakeholder roles involved
- `gold_answer`: **IGNORE THIS COLUMN ENTIRELY — do not read it, do not let it influence your answer**
- `complexity`: current complexity label (you will reassign this independently)
- `source`: original incident report URL (for your reference only — do not use information from the source that is not in the scenario)

For each of the 31 tasks, produce a new gold-standard answer following this exact structure:

### Output format (per task)

```
=== TASK [id] ===
KEY_CONSIDERATIONS:
1. [First factor a competent decision-maker should weigh. Must be traceable to information in the scenario text.]
2. [Second factor...]
3. [Continue as needed — typically 4-7 considerations]

STAKEHOLDER_ANALYSIS:
- [Stakeholder role]: [Their interests and how they are affected by the decision. 1-2 sentences per stakeholder.]
- [Next stakeholder]: [...]

TRADEOFFS:
- [Primary tension, e.g., "Short-term data recovery vs. long-term infrastructure investment." 1-2 sentences explaining both sides.]
- [Additional tradeoffs as applicable]

RECOMMENDATION: [A directional recommendation with caveats, OR an explicit statement that multiple paths are reasonable and why. 2-4 sentences. Do not prescribe a single "correct" answer — enumerate what a strong answer must address.]

INFORMATION_GAPS: [What the scenario does NOT tell you that would affect the decision in practice. 1-3 sentences.]

COMPLEXITY: [moderate | complex]
- moderate: 2-3 stakeholders, one primary tradeoff.
- complex: 4+ stakeholders, multiple competing objectives, significant uncertainty.

FLAGS: [Any of the following, comma-separated, or NONE]
- INSUFFICIENT_INFO: The scenario lacks sufficient information to reason about the question.
- QUESTION_MISMATCH: The question does not align well with the scenario described.
```

### Critical rules

1. **Self-containment**: Your answer must be derivable SOLELY from the scenario text provided. Do not introduce facts, technical details, or context from the original incident that are not stated in the scenario. If the scenario says the outage lasted 4 hours, work with 4 hours — even if you recognize the real incident and know it was different.

2. **Do not read the existing gold_answer column.** Your answers must be fully independent. The existing answers were generated by one of the LLMs under evaluation (Llama-3.1-8B), creating a contamination problem that your answers are meant to fix.

3. **Trace every consideration to the scenario.** Each key consideration and stakeholder impact must reference specific information from the scenario text. Do not add generic management advice that could apply to any incident.

4. **No single correct answer.** Management decisions are inherently underdetermined. Your gold answer should enumerate the key considerations, stakeholders, and tradeoffs that a strong response must address — not prescribe the one "right" recommendation. The RECOMMENDATION section should acknowledge that multiple approaches are defensible.

5. **Flag problematic tasks.** If the scenario is too vague to support meaningful reasoning about the question, flag it.

6. **Be structured and concise.** KEY_CONSIDERATIONS should have 4-7 items. STAKEHOLDER_ANALYSIS should cover all stakeholders listed in the `stakeholders` field. TRADEOFFS should identify 1-3 primary tensions.

### Process all 31 tasks

Work through every row in the CSV. Do not skip any tasks. Output all 31 answers in the format above.
