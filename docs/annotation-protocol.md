# Annotation Protocol: Gold-Standard Authoring and Output Scoring

**Study:** Formalization Gradient — Empirical Effects of Representation Formality on Local LLM Performance
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** 2026-03-17

---

## 1. Purpose

This protocol governs two annotation activities:

| Activity | Goal | Levels |
|----------|------|--------|
| **A. Gold-standard authoring** | Replace LLM-generated gold answers with independently authored expert answers | Semi-formal (64 tasks), Low-formal (31 tasks) |
| **B. Output quality scoring** | Score LLM outputs on a unified 0–3 rubric | All three levels (high, semi, low) |

**Why this is needed:** The current gold standards for semi-formal and low-formal tasks were generated by Llama-3.1-8B, one of the two models under evaluation. This creates a self-referential bias in recall-based overlap metrics. Independently authored gold standards are required for publication.

---

## 2. General Principles

1. **Self-containment rule.** Every gold answer must be derivable solely from the information provided in the task input (clause text, scenario, schema). Do not use external knowledge, legal databases, case law, or domain expertise beyond what the text states.
2. **No access to LLM outputs.** Annotators must not see any LLM-generated answers (neither the current gold standards nor model outputs) before completing their own gold answer. This prevents anchoring bias.
3. **Independent work.** Each annotator works independently. Do not discuss specific tasks with other annotators until calibration sessions.
4. **Record keeping.** Every annotation must include: annotator ID, task ID, timestamp (ISO 8601), and any free-text notes about ambiguity or difficulty.

## 3. Activity A: Gold-Standard Authoring

### 3.1 Semi-Formal Tasks (Legal Clause Interpretation)

**Annotator profile:** Legal domain expertise (contract law, commercial agreements). Law degree or equivalent professional experience with contract interpretation.

**Number of tasks:** 64
**Annotators required:** 2 (independent, for inter-annotator agreement)

#### Input provided per task

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `clause_text` | Verbatim contract clause from CUAD (authentic legal language) |
| `scenario` | A factual situation where the clause's meaning is tested |
| `question` | A specific question about what the clause permits, requires, or prohibits |

**Note:** The `scenario` and `question` fields were LLM-generated but have passed automated validation (self-containment, no external references, answerability). Annotators should flag any scenario or question that is implausible, unanswerable from the clause, or internally inconsistent — but the primary task is writing the gold answer.

#### Instructions

For each task:

1. **Read** the clause text carefully. Identify defined terms, conditions, exceptions, and temporal constraints.
2. **Read** the scenario and question.
3. **Flag** the task if:
   - The scenario introduces facts not grounded in the clause (mark: `FLAG:SCENARIO_UNGROUNDED`)
   - The question cannot be answered from the clause text alone (mark: `FLAG:UNANSWERABLE`)
   - The clause contains unresolvable external references (e.g., "as defined in Schedule A") that make the question unanswerable (mark: `FLAG:EXTERNAL_REF`)
4. **Write the gold answer.** Structure as follows:
   - **Conclusion:** Direct answer to the question (permitted / prohibited / required / conditional / ambiguous).
   - **Reasoning:** Which specific provisions of the clause support this conclusion. Quote or paraphrase the relevant language.
   - **Limitations:** What the clause does NOT resolve. If the clause is silent on a material point raised by the scenario, state this explicitly.
5. **Assign complexity:**
   - `simple` — Single applicable provision, straightforward application to the scenario.
   - `moderate` — Multiple provisions interact, or a condition must be evaluated.
   - `complex` — Ambiguity, competing provisions, exceptions that may or may not apply, or temporal/conditional reasoning required.
6. **Assign ambiguity flag:**
   - `YES` — The clause leaves a material question raised by the scenario unanswered.
   - `NO` — The clause fully resolves the question.

#### Output format (per task)

```
task_id:        [integer]
annotator_id:   [string]
timestamp:      [ISO 8601]
gold_answer:    [free text, structured as Conclusion / Reasoning / Limitations]
complexity:     [simple | moderate | complex]
ambiguity_flag: [YES | NO]
flags:          [comma-separated flag codes, or NONE]
notes:          [free text, optional]
```

### 3.2 Low-Formal Tasks (Management Decision-Making)

**Annotator profile:** Management, organizational behavior, or incident response expertise. Experience with operational decision-making under uncertainty.

**Number of tasks:** 31
**Annotators required:** 2 (independent)

#### Input provided per task

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| `scenario` | A fictionalized software/operations incident derived from a real postmortem |
| `stakeholders` | Named stakeholder roles involved |
| `question` | A management decision question |

#### Instructions

For each task:

1. **Read** the scenario, stakeholder list, and question.
2. **Flag** if the scenario lacks sufficient information to reason about the question (mark: `FLAG:INSUFFICIENT_INFO`).
3. **Write the gold answer.** Structure as follows:
   - **Key considerations:** Enumerate the factors a competent decision-maker should weigh. Each consideration should be traceable to information in the scenario text.
   - **Stakeholder analysis:** For each relevant stakeholder, identify their interests and how they are affected.
   - **Tradeoffs:** Identify the primary tensions (e.g., short-term cost vs. long-term risk, speed vs. safety).
   - **Recommendation (optional):** If the scenario supports a directional recommendation, state it with caveats. If multiple reasonable paths exist, say so.
   - **What the scenario does NOT tell you:** Explicitly list information gaps that would affect the decision in practice.
4. **Assign complexity:**
   - `moderate` — 2–3 stakeholders, one primary tradeoff.
   - `complex` — 4+ stakeholders, multiple competing objectives, significant uncertainty.

#### Output format (per task)

```
task_id:        [integer]
annotator_id:   [string]
timestamp:      [ISO 8601]
gold_answer:    [free text, structured as above]
complexity:     [moderate | complex]
flags:          [comma-separated flag codes, or NONE]
notes:          [free text, optional]
```

## 4. Activity B: Output Quality Scoring (0–3 Rubric)

**Annotator profile:** Domain-appropriate expertise per level (see below).
**Annotators required:** 2 per output (independent scoring)

### 4.1 Rubric

| Score | Label | Criteria |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **3** | Excellent | Correct and complete. SQL: executes correctly, returns right result set. Legal: identifies all applicable provisions, reaches correct conclusion. Management: covers all key stakeholders and tradeoffs in the gold standard. |
| **2** | Adequate | Substantially correct with minor gaps. Misses secondary considerations but captures the core answer. |
| **1** | Partial | Captures some relevant content but misses major elements or contains significant errors. |
| **0** | Incorrect | Wrong answer, irrelevant content, or failure to address the task. |

### 4.2 Level-Specific Scoring Guidance

#### High-formal (SQL)

- **Annotator profile:** Database expertise (SQL fluency, relational schema comprehension).
- **Scoring basis:** Compare model-generated SQL against the gold-standard query AND the schema.
- **Score 3:** Query is syntactically valid, logically equivalent to the gold standard, and would return the correct result set when executed against the schema.
- **Score 2:** Query captures the main logic but has minor issues (e.g., missing an ORDER BY, includes an unnecessary column).
- **Score 1:** Query addresses the right tables but has wrong joins, missing conditions, or incorrect aggregation.
- **Score 0:** Query is syntactically invalid, queries wrong tables, or does not address the question.

#### Semi-formal (Legal)

- **Annotator profile:** Legal domain expertise.
- **Scoring basis:** Compare model output against the independently authored gold answer (from Activity A).
- **Score 3:** Identifies all applicable provisions, reaches the correct conclusion, and notes relevant limitations.
- **Score 2:** Correct conclusion but misses a secondary provision or limitation.
- **Score 1:** Partially correct — identifies some relevant provisions but misses the main point or makes an error in reasoning.
- **Score 0:** Wrong conclusion, cites irrelevant provisions, or fails to engage with the clause.

#### Low-formal (Management)

- **Annotator profile:** Management / organizational expertise.
- **Scoring basis:** Compare model output against the independently authored gold answer (from Activity A).
- **Score 3:** Covers all key considerations, identifies relevant stakeholders and tradeoffs.
- **Score 2:** Covers most key considerations, misses one secondary stakeholder or tradeoff.
- **Score 1:** Addresses the topic but misses major considerations or stakeholders.
- **Score 0:** Off-topic, superficial platitudes without engaging the scenario specifics, or factually inconsistent with the scenario.

### 4.3 Sampling Strategy

**Full annotation** (preferred): Score all 1,949 model outputs (195 tasks × 2 models × 5 runs).

**Stratified sample** (minimum viable): Score a balanced subset:
- 30 tasks per level (90 total)
- Balanced across: models (Llama/Mistral), complexity tags, run indices
- = 90 tasks × 2 models × 5 runs = 900 outputs
- Each scored by 2 annotators = 1,800 scoring judgments

### 4.4 Output format (per scored output)

```
task_id:        [integer]
model:          [llama | mistral]
run_index:      [0-4]
annotator_id:   [string]
timestamp:      [ISO 8601]
score:          [0 | 1 | 2 | 3]
notes:          [free text, optional — especially for scores of 1 where the boundary is ambiguous]
```

## 5. Calibration Procedure

Before full annotation begins:

1. **Calibration set:** Select 5 tasks per level (15 total). Both annotators independently score the same outputs.
2. **Compute agreement:** Calculate Cohen's kappa on the calibration set. Target: κ ≥ 0.70.
3. **Reconciliation session:** Annotators discuss disagreements, clarify rubric interpretation, and resolve edge cases.
4. **Repeat if needed:** If κ < 0.70 after calibration, refine scoring guidance and repeat with a fresh calibration set.
5. **Document calibration decisions:** Any clarifications or edge-case rulings from the reconciliation session become binding addenda to this protocol.

---

## 6. Sequencing

| Step | Activity | Dependency | Deliverable |
|------|----------|------------|-------------|
| 1 | Gold-standard authoring (semi-formal) | None | 64 expert gold answers × 2 annotators |
| 2 | Gold-standard authoring (low-formal) | None | 31 expert gold answers × 2 annotators |
| 3 | Gold-standard reconciliation | Steps 1–2 | Consensus gold answers (resolve disagreements) |
| 4 | Re-run overlap metrics | Step 3 | Updated quality scores against new gold standards |
| 5 | Calibration round (rubric scoring) | Step 3 | κ ≥ 0.70 confirmed |
| 6 | Full rubric scoring | Step 5 | Scored outputs + inter-annotator agreement |

Steps 1 and 2 can run in parallel (different annotator pools).
Step 4 is automated (re-run existing evaluation scripts against new gold CSVs).
