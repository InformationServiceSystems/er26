## Prompt

You are an expert legal analyst generating gold-standard answers for a scientific study. The study measures how well LLMs interpret legal contract clauses at different levels of task formalization.

I am uploading a CSV file with 64 legal clause interpretation tasks. Each row has these fields:
- `id`: task identifier
- `label`: clause category (e.g., Anti-Assignment, Termination For Convenience)
- `clause_text`: verbatim contract clause from a real commercial agreement (CUAD dataset)
- `scenario`: a factual situation where the clause's meaning is tested
- `question`: a specific question about what the clause permits, requires, or prohibits
- `gold_answer`: **IGNORE THIS COLUMN ENTIRELY — do not read it, do not let it influence your answer**
- `complexity`: current complexity label (you will reassign this independently)
- `ambiguity_flag`: current flag (you will reassign this independently)

For each of the 64 tasks, produce a new gold-standard answer following this exact structure:

### Output format (per task)

```
=== TASK [id] ===
CONCLUSION: [Direct answer: PERMITTED / PROHIBITED / REQUIRED / CONDITIONAL / AMBIGUOUS. One sentence stating the bottom line.]

REASONING: [Which specific provisions of the clause support this conclusion. Quote or closely paraphrase the relevant clause language. Cite specific conditions, exceptions, definitions, and temporal constraints. 3-6 sentences.]

LIMITATIONS: [What the clause does NOT resolve. If the clause is silent on a material point raised by the scenario, state this explicitly. If the clause fully resolves the question, write "None — the clause fully resolves this question." 1-3 sentences.]

COMPLEXITY: [simple | moderate | complex]
- simple: Single applicable provision, straightforward application to the scenario.
- moderate: Multiple provisions interact, or a condition must be evaluated.
- complex: Ambiguity, competing provisions, exceptions that may or may not apply, or temporal/conditional reasoning required.

AMBIGUITY_FLAG: [YES | NO]
- YES: The clause leaves a material question raised by the scenario unanswered.
- NO: The clause fully resolves the question.

FLAGS: [Any of the following, comma-separated, or NONE]
- SCENARIO_UNGROUNDED: The scenario introduces facts not derivable from the clause text.
- UNANSWERABLE: The question cannot be answered from the clause text alone.
- EXTERNAL_REF: The clause references external documents (schedules, exhibits, appendices) needed to answer the question.
```

### Critical rules

1. **Self-containment**: Your answer must be derivable SOLELY from the clause text provided. Do not use external legal knowledge, case law, statutory interpretation, or general legal principles beyond what the clause states. If the clause says X, your answer is based on X — even if standard legal practice would differ.

2. **Do not read the existing gold_answer column.** Your answers must be fully independent. The existing answers were generated by one of the LLMs under evaluation, creating a contamination problem that your answers are meant to fix.

3. **Quote the clause.** When reasoning, reference specific language from the clause (e.g., "The clause states: 'without the prior written consent of the other Party'"). This makes it verifiable that your answer derives from the text.

4. **Acknowledge ambiguity honestly.** If the clause is genuinely ambiguous on the question asked, say so. Do not force a definitive answer where the text does not support one. Use CONCLUSION: AMBIGUOUS in such cases.

5. **Flag problematic tasks.** If the scenario introduces facts the clause cannot address, or the question requires information not in the clause, flag it. These flags help us identify tasks that need revision.

6. **Be thorough but concise.** REASONING should be 3-6 sentences. Do not write a legal memorandum — write a clear, structured expert answer.

### Process all 64 tasks

Work through every row in the CSV. Do not skip any tasks. Output all 64 answers in the format above.
