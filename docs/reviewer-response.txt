================================================================================
RESPONSE TO REVIEWERS
Submission 51: "Formalization Matters"
================================================================================

We thank the meta-reviewer and all three reviewers for their careful and
detailed reading. The reviews identified several substantive methodological
weaknesses, and we have revised the paper accordingly. This letter summarizes
the major changes and then responds to each reviewer point by point. Where a
concern could not be fully resolved within the current study design, we say so
candidly rather than overstate what the revision achieves.

Throughout, section and table numbers refer to the revised manuscript.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
SUMMARY OF MAJOR CHANGES
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. Execution-based SQL evaluation (Reviewer 1's central objection). The
   high-formal level is now scored with Spider-style execution accuracy: gold
   and predicted queries are executed against a populated Northwind database and
   result sets compared exactly. New Section "High-Formal Execution Accuracy"
   and Table (tab:sql-exec). This corroborates the overlap-based model ranking
   on an objective metric and yields an execution-grounded within-domain
   difficulty gradient.

2. Formalization metric F(T) operationalized per instance and validated. F(T)
   is now computed per task (not merely per level); a nested-model ablation
   shows it predicts quality BEYOND the categorical high/semi/low encoding
   (new Section "Validating the Formalization Metric F(T)", Table tab:F-dims),
   including a circularity control.

3. Precision-sensitive metrics added. We now report precision and F1 alongside
   recall (Table tab:precision); this reveals that the recall-based inter-model
   ranking is a verbosity artifact that reverses under precision.

4. Variance analysis corrected. We decompose across-run from across-task
   variance (Table tab:variance-decomp) and show the variance paradox holds for
   the across-run quantity the hypothesis actually names.

5. Unified evaluation representation. All overlap metrics are recomputed on a
   single raw-text word-set representation, removing a prior inconsistency.

6. Temperature-robustness experiment. We re-ran the full benchmark at
   T in {0, 0.3, 0.7, 1.0} (new Section "Robustness to Sampling Temperature",
   Table tab:temp-sweep). The variance paradox and the dominant quality drop
   persist at every temperature for both models.

7. Framing and presentation. Revised title and an explicit scope statement;
   expanded related work on AI-assisted conceptual modeling; hedged causal and
   design claims; corrected wording.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
RESPONSE TO THE META-REVIEW
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The meta-review consolidated four blocking concerns.

(a) Evaluation methodology for the high-formal SQL task.
    Addressed. We added execution-based evaluation (Spider-style exact
    result-set match against a populated Northwind database; Section
    "High-Formal Execution Accuracy", Table tab:sql-exec) and precision/F1
    metrics (Table tab:precision). Overlap metrics are retained only as the
    cross-level common denominator, with the objective execution metric
    reported for the level where correctness is decidable.

(b) Domain-formalization confound.
    Partially addressed, and now discussed candidly. We strengthened the
    within-domain analysis (which now includes the execution-grounded SQL
    difficulty gradient) and added the per-instance F(T) validation, both of
    which show the gradient operating within a single domain. We also hedged
    the causal language throughout. We are explicit in the Limitations that the
    three-domain design cannot fully isolate formalization from domain, and we
    frame the cross-level results as associational.

(c) Study repeatability and generalizability.
    Addressed for temperature (full T in {0,0.3,0.7,1.0} sweep; the findings
    persist). We release all task data, runners, evaluators, and analysis
    scripts, and report the fixed protocol (K=5 repeats, documented seeds and
    settings). Generalization to substantially larger (70B+) models is
    identified as future work.

(d) Validity of LLM-generated gold standards.
    Partially addressed. The disclosure is moved from a footnote into the main
    text (Robustness and Validity Checks). We explain the family-independence
    rationale (semi/low gold produced by a model from neither evaluated family)
    and the self-containment constraints on the annotation prompts. A unified
    0-3 expert rubric and a stratified annotation sample have been prepared;
    the two-annotator scoring is in progress and is the remaining step.

RESPONSE TO REVIEWER 1
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

R1.1 "Overlap metrics for SQL instead of execution/AST equivalence; this
      undermines the strongest evidence for H1 and affects the variance
      conclusions."
    FIXED. We added execution-based evaluation for the high-formal level
    (Section "High-Formal Execution Accuracy", Table tab:sql-exec). Gold and
    predicted SQL are executed against a populated Northwind database (DuckDB)
    and result sets compared exactly (order-insensitive unless ORDER BY). Under
    this objective metric, Mistral scores 49% and Llama 61% exact result-set
    accuracy over the 95 gradeable tasks (five gold queries use constructs
    outside the engine's dialect and are excluded). Two points now reinforced
    in the paper: (i) the execution ranking agrees with the overlap and F1
    rankings, so the model comparison is not a proxy artifact; and (ii)
    accuracy declines with query difficulty (easy>medium>hard), an
    execution-grounded within-domain gradient.

R1.2 "Variance analysis may conflate across-run variability with across-task
      dispersion."
    FIXED. H2 concerns across-RUN variance, but the pooled per-record variance
    also absorbs between-task dispersion. We now decompose the two
    (Table tab:variance-decomp): within-task (across the K=5 runs of a task)
    versus between-task (variance of per-task means). We acknowledge the
    original pooled test was dominated by between-task dispersion, but show that
    the across-run variance -- the quantity H2 names -- exhibits the same
    monotonic decrease with formalization (Kruskal-Wallis p = 7.9e-12), so the
    refutation of H2 is robust to the correction.

R1.3 "F(T) not instantiated and validated quantitatively; unclear whether it is
      per-instance or per-level."
    FIXED. F(T) is now computed PER TASK INSTANCE from task metadata and
    gold-output structural features (Section "Method"; Table tab:F-dims). We add
    a nested-model ablation over all 190 tasks (Section "Validating the
    Formalization Metric F(T)"): F(T) significantly improves fit over the
    categorical level encoding (nested F = 9.31, p = 0.0026), confirming it
    carries information beyond the three labels. We also report within-level
    correlations and a circularity control that partials out gold-text features;
    we treat the cross-level ablation as primary and report the within-level
    signal with this caveat.

R1.4 "Semi- and low-formal gold standards are LLM-generated; expert validation
      is incomplete."
    Partially addressed. The provenance is now discussed in the main text
    (Robustness and Validity Checks) with the family-independence rationale and
    the self-containment constraints on the generation prompts. A unified 0-3
    expert rubric and a stratified sample are prepared; the two-annotator
    scoring is in progress and is stated as the remaining validation step.

R1.5 "Expand precision-sensitive metrics."
    FIXED. Precision and F1 are reported alongside recall (Table tab:precision).
    They show that Llama's high-formal recall advantage is driven by verbosity
    and reverses under precision (Mistral has higher precision and F1); H3
    (length-quality mediation) is accordingly now supported with a demonstrated
    mechanism rather than a conjecture.

RESPONSE TO REVIEWER 2
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

R2.1 "Title and framing imply all three tasks involve explicit conceptual
      modeling; only the SQL task does."
    FIXED. The title no longer claims conceptual modeling across all tasks. We
    added an explicit scope statement in the Introduction: only the high-formal
    task operates over an explicit conceptual model (the relational schema); the
    semi- and low-formal tasks are reasoning tasks over progressively less
    formal representations. The study measures how representation formality
    affects LLM outputs, not how LLMs construct conceptual models.

R2.2 "Hypotheses appear disconnected from the research problem."
    Partially addressed. We added the scope framing above and made the
    continuous-dimension contribution explicit in the Introduction so the
    hypotheses follow more naturally from the identified gaps. We did not
    fully restructure the Introduction-to-hypotheses flow and can do so if the
    reviewer prefers.

R2.3 "Domain-formalization confound is a fundamental threat to the central
      causal claim."
    Partially addressed, and now stated candidly. We strengthened the
    within-domain evidence (SQL difficulty gradient, now also on execution
    accuracy; management complexity gradient) and the per-instance F(T)
    validation, both of which show the gradient within a single domain. We
    hedged all causal language (e.g., "formalization is associated with quality
    beyond domain alone") and state plainly in the Limitations that the
    three-domain design cannot fully isolate formalization from domain. We agree
    a same-domain design is the definitive remedy; we note it as the principal
    direction for future work.

R2.4 "Gold-standard validity concern must move from a footnote to the main text
      and be addressed with rigor."
    Addressed. The disclosure is now in the main text (Robustness and Validity
    Checks), with the model-family-independence argument, the self-containment
    constraints, and the planned expert rubric. Full expert validation is in
    progress.

R2.5 "F(T) is not anchored to the data in the tables; Results contains
      methodological content."
    Addressed for F(T): its three dimensions are now anchored to concrete
    per-level values in Table tab:F-dims and validated in a dedicated section.
    We also moved representation-choice details into the Method. We reviewed the
    Method/Results boundary and can relocate further material if desired.

R2.6 "State the continuous-dimension treatment explicitly as a contribution."
    FIXED. The Introduction now lists, as a contribution, that we treat
    formalization as a continuous dimension measured at three points via a
    per-instance metric shown to predict quality beyond the categorical labels.

R2.7 (p11) "Clarify whether the data show verification/retry mechanisms would be
      sufficient."
    FIXED. We now state that the data establish the NEED for verification (the
    bimodal distribution and 49-61% execution accuracy) but do not establish
    that any particular mitigation is sufficient; verification and retry-and-
    select are framed as advisable design heuristics warranting further study,
    not proven remedies.

R2.8 (p11) "The claim that formalization rather than domain influences quality
      is undermined by the confound and should be hedged."
    FIXED. Hedged as described in R2.3.

R2.9 (p14) "'This research investigated' should attribute agency to the
      researchers."
    FIXED. Changed to "The authors investigated."

RESPONSE TO REVIEWER 3
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

R3.1 "Related work: remove the general description of conceptual modeling; add
      more from AI-assisted modeling; strengthen justification via comparative
      analysis."
    FIXED. We added a paragraph on LLM-assisted conceptual modeling (Camara et
    al., Fill et al., Harer, and recent ER-community work) and positioned our
    contribution comparatively: prior work adopts a generation framing and
    evaluates ad hoc, whereas we provide a controlled measurement of how the
    formalization level of the representation affects output quality and
    consistency, which informs when those generation methods can be trusted.

R3.2 "Specify the rationale and scientific grounds of the experiment design and
      its robustness; clarify repeatability and generalization."
    Addressed. The design rationale (self-contained inputs, gold standards, and
    graded complexity as invariants that isolate the formalization variable) is
    stated in the Method. For robustness/generalizability we added the
    execution-based evaluation and the temperature sweep (Table tab:temp-sweep;
    both central findings persist across T in {0,0.3,0.7,1.0}). For
    repeatability we release the datasets, runners, evaluators, and analysis
    scripts and report the fixed generation protocol (K=5, temperature, token
    caps). Larger-model generalization is noted as future work.

R3.3 "Theoretical implications; translation into actionable artifacts."
    Partially addressed. We frame F(T) as an a-priori risk estimator for
    LLM-assisted modeling (higher formalization predicts higher expected quality
    but wider variance), locate the reliability boundary at the high-to-semi
    transition, and release the benchmark and the F(T)/evaluation tooling as
    reusable artifacts. A deeper theoretical treatment is an avenue we are happy
    to expand.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
REMAINING LIMITATIONS (STATED HONESTLY)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

- Expert rubric annotation is prepared (unified 0-3 rubric, stratified sample)
  but the two-annotator scoring is not yet complete; until it is, the semi- and
  low-formal results rest on overlap metrics against LLM-generated references.
- The cross-domain confound is mitigated (within-domain gradients, F(T),
  execution grounding) and candidly discussed, but not eliminated by the
  three-domain design; a same-domain formalization gradient is the definitive
  remedy and our main future direction.
- Generalization to 70B+ models is not evaluated.

We believe the revision substantially strengthens the empirical evidence,
particularly by replacing the overlap proxy with execution-based evaluation at
the high-formal level, validating F(T) as more than a relabeling of the three
levels, correcting the variance analysis, and demonstrating that the central
findings persist across sampling temperatures. We thank the reviewers again for
feedback that materially improved the paper.
